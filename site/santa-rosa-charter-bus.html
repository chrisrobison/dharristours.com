Visit the Charles M Schultz Museum

As the home of the Charles M. Schultz Museum, the city of Santa Rosa is well known for Peanuts fans who can't get enough of Charlie Brown and the Gang. The museum features numerous displays and exhibits about the beginning of the Peanuts cartoon strip and the man who started it all. The Museum features exhibits such as a real-life replica of the artist's studio just as it was while writing the comic strip and advertisements and banners from years of Peanuts marketing programs.

The Schultz Museum is a wonderful location for a school trip, church outing or even just a day trip for families. DHarris Tours has the perfect transportation to meet your group's needs while planning a trip to the Schultz Museum or anywhere else around Santa Rosa . 

Experience the Pacific Coast Air Museum

If you love learning about the history of airplanes, take a look at the Pacific Coast Air Museum. Featuring plenty of educational exhibits and events, the Air Museum includes 35 Vintage Aircraft along with special events like the Mustang Round Up and the Air Show. There are a number of classes and tours that provide you with information about the history of the planes along with details about the principles of flight and how to develop your own aviation career.

The Pacific Coast Air Museum also includes open cockpit weekends where visitors can learn more about fighter planes like the F-5E Tiger II, the F-16N Viper, and the F-14A Tomcat. These historic airplanes along with others give visitors a close up look at the environment inside the planes and what pilots experience in flight. The open cockpit weekends also showcase the famous Topgun training school and the training pilots have to endure.

Luther Burbank Home and Gardens

If the study of Horticulture and the development of plants interests you, then the Luther Burbank Home and Gardens is definitely the place for you. As a renowned Horticulturist, Luther Burbank spent 50 years learning about how to develop different types of plants and flowers. The Burbank home in Santa Rosa has been turned into a full-fledged museum from the house and property to the greenhouses where he worked and developed different varieties of plants.

The Luther Burbank Home and Gardens includes group and drop-in tours, educational lectures and classes for children, information about the various gardens on the property as well as the fruit trees and specialty plants. The Carriage House Museum also shows the area where Luther Burbank spent many hours in research and development. One of Burbank's special projects was in trying to develop the perfect Plum as well as other fruits.

Railroad Square Historic District

One of the most interesting areas of Santa Rosa is the Railroad Square Historic District. Located in the heart of the city, the historic district was developed in 1979 as a preservation project of the "Old Town" and was developed around the old railroad depot of the 1870's. The Railroad Square features several antique shops, cafes, hotels and entertainment venues for you to enjoy.

The Railroad Square Historic District was originally known as "Little Italy" because of its large population of Italian immigrants who settled in the area. It soon developed into an agricultural hub with wine vineyards becoming a major part of the area. Many of the original buildings in the square were used as canneries and warehouses for the local crops as well as breweries and rail supporting industries. The Railroad Square Historic District is a great place to visit if you want to learn about the early history of the city.

Sonoma Wine Tours

You can't visit the area without taking one of the popular Sonoma Wine Tours. Sonoma County is known for its many vineyards and wineries and there are several wineries that provide tours and tastings. The Sonoma Wine Tours includes private guided tours along with tastings and meals. Sonoma Wine Tours gives you a customized tour of many local family-owned and operated wineries, including Benziger, Deerfield Ranch, Kunde, and Landmark Vineyards.
