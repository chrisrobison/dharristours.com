Explore Modesto

Landmarks: The McHenry Mansion is perhaps the most popular location in the city. It was built in 1882 by Robert McHenry, a wealthy banker and businessman. McHenry Mansion is one of the city's only remaining Victorian homes and is toured by over 20,000 people each year.

Recreation: The State Theatre has been around for over 80 years and is beloved by the city for its unique architectural design. It began as a movie theater but has since expanded to include a stage and a community center. It is currently owned by a nonprofit organization that shows classic films like "The Sound of Music" and "The Lion King." There are a lot of special events at the theater for kids and seniors.

Baseball lovers will definitely want to check out John Thurman Field, home of the minor league Modesto Nuts. The team has a long history that stretches all the way back to 1946. They are currently a "farm team" for the Colorado Rockies and have produced a number of hall of fame players, including Reggie Jackson and Joe Morgan.

Culture: The Great Valley Museum is the perfect place for a field trip. It is connected to a junior college and features native habitat displays of the California Valley. There's a planetarium, an observatory, a discovery room, and more! They have a teacher outreach program to help schools use the facility to aid the development of students.

Those looking for a great place to bike or walk need to visit the Tuolumne River Trail. This path follows the Tuolumne River and winds through a thick forest. It is a very scenic and it is very popular among the locals.
