Reserve a Few Days for Your Oakland Trip

The city deserves at least a few days of your time to really impress, and tell you its story. These attractions certainly contribute to Oakland's image as a tourist destination:

Landmarks and Museums: Jack London Square is the place where all tourists meet because this is the city's number one attraction, mostly visited now for its beautiful buildings, stores and restaurants. Don't hesitate to visit the SS Potomac, a ship available for dockside tours with plenty of interesting stories to tell, especially about its time as Franklin Delano Roosevelt's presidential yacht. The Oakland Museum of California focuses its collections on the art and history of California which are housed inside a superb building. You should also visit the Peralta Museum of History and Culture, and the house of Antonio Peralta, where you can admire all the exhibits and a native plant garden.

Parks and Gardens: The city is rich in beautiful parks and gardens, with Joaquin Miller Park being one of the most beautiful parks in town. It is located on the Oakland hills and is a great destination for hiking and biking. The Preservation Park enchants visitors with its 19th century Victorian-era homes, while the GSBF Bonsai Garden displays over 100 bonsai trees.

Attractions for Kids: The city has numerous attractions reserved for kids, but the most visited ones are without any doubt the Chabot Space and Science Center, where the little ones can watch planetarium shows and Megadome movies, fly into space and learn about our Universe. The Children's Fairyland is a playground situated on Lake Merritt, rich in fun things to do, and you should also plan a trip to the Oakland Zoo.

Day Trips: Groups in Oakland also enjoy traveling to a number of surrounding cities such as San Francisco, Las Vegas, Sacramento, Modesto. DHarris Tours's services accomodate passengers looking to travel to cities anywhere in the nation.
